Research

In Pacific Island Countries, the environmental resources that support livelihoods are distributed across landscapes in a mix of spatial patterns. Capturing the spatial detail in landscape use is important to inform landscape management that is sensitve to these livelihood dependencies.

This research combines a conceptual framework that considers links between livelihoods and the environment (Environmental Livelihood Security) with a collaborative software development methodology (ICT4D) to:

  • develop a workflow and geospatial apps to map and monitor landscapes.
  • build the capacity of landscape stakeholders to use GIS and geospatial data for landscape management.
