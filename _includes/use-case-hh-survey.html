Household Survey - Fiji

Estimating Coastal Landscape Service Flows

A household survey including closed and open questions covering topics related to household liveihood activities, use of landscape resources, and climatic and other challenges related to landscape resource use was undertaken in four communities in Coastal Ba, Fiji - Etatoko, Koronubu Vunibaka, Natutu, and Nawaqarua. To collect the data, a digital data collection form was developed using Open Data Kit (ODK). The household survey data was analysed by representing coastal livelihood systems using network graphs that visualise:

  • How different people undertake different landscape activities (i.e. varied forms of livelihood diversity)
  • The varying value of flows of ecosystem services from different landscape activities
  • The varied impacts of climate shocks to landscape activities
These network graphs are drawn separately at the household level and for men and women landscape users.
