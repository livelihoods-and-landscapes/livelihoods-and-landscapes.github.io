Geospatial applications for climate-smart landscape management

Through a collaborative information and communications technology for development (ICT4D) process, we are developing open-source mobile, desktop, and cloud tools to monitor and map diverse landscapes and seascapes.

This project is funded by the Australian Centre for International Agricultural Research (ACIAR) and is a collaboration between stakeholders in Fiji, Tonga, Australia, and New Zealand. We are testing and developing geospatial applications in diverse landscapes and ecosystems across Fiji and Tonga.

  • map landscape resources in the field using mobile GIS
  • monitor inter-annual landscape dynamics using satellite images
  • capture rich socio-economic and agricultural information
  • spatial dashboards to integrate field data with secondary geospatial data layers
  • spatial databases and server-side applications to manage diverse datasets

Research

In Pacific Island Countries, the environmental resources that support livelihoods are distributed across the landscape in a mix of spatial patterns. Capturing the spatial detail in landscape use is important to: i) generate an accurate understanding of the diverse ways landscape resources are used to support livelihoods, and ii) inform management of landscape resources that is sensitve to these livelihood dependencies.

This research combines a conceptual framework that considers links between livelihoods, the environment, and external stressors (Environmental Livelihood Security (ELS)) and a collaborative software development methodology (ICT4D).

Through this process we aim to identify and develop geospatial applications to capture and analyse the diverse ways landscapes are used to support livelihoods, are aligned with landscape stakeholder's needs and workflows, and builds the capacity of landscape stakeholders to use GIS and geospatial data when managing landscape resources.

The geospatial applications and workflows developed here provide tools to:

  • capture geospatial data that reflects the diverse (e.g. farms, mangroves, rivers), local, and multi-scale uses of landscape resources (e.g. kitchen gardens, large commercial farms)
  • integrate spatial data collected “in the field” with (big) secondary geospatial layers
  • easily process, analyse, and visualise local-level spatial data collected “in the field” using mobile GIS
  • enable non-GIS specialists, across socio-cultural contexts, to generate insights from geospatial data relevant for managing landscape resources

Training and capacity building

We develop training materials and deliver workshops to build the capacity of landscape stakeholders to use GIS applications to collect and analyse geospatial data and to use this data to inform landscape decision making and management.

All training materials and tutorials used in these workshops are freely available online.

Training Website

In Fiji and Tonga, we have run training sessions and workshops with officials from various government ministries, NGOs, and other landscape stakeholders.
